Business Plan for FlareLink v1

Overview

Hi Mum and Dad, I’m excited to share my business idea with you! I’m launching a product called FlareLink v1. It’s a specialized tool that helps individuals interested in cybersecurity and ethical technology analysis. The FlareLink v1 device comes with a touchscreen, a protective case, a stylus, a charger, and all the necessary cables—ready to go with pre-installed Marauder software for WiFi and Bluetooth analysis.

The FlareLink v1 is designed for those who want to explore networking, security, and digital privacy in a legal, responsible way. By offering an affordable, beginner-friendly device, I’m helping people learn how to analyze wireless networks and Bluetooth signals, which is essential knowledge for anyone wanting to enter the field of cybersecurity.

The product comes in two options:

  • With firmware installed: £42 (excluding shipping)
  • Without firmware installed: £40 (excluding shipping)

Shipping is £7 for one device and £9 for two. So for one device, the total will be £49 (£42 + £7 shipping), and for two devices, the total will be £59 (£40 each + £9 shipping).

Prototyping and Stock

I plan to start small by buying just 2 units to test the market. The first units will come with everything pre-built and ready to use, with the Marauder firmware installed for one and not installed for the other (at a lower price). The initial cost for these 2 units will be around £84 (£42 for the pre-installed firmware unit and £40 for the unit without).

  • Initial Investment: £84 for the first 2 units, which I will fund using my birthday money.
  • Low Risk: Since I’m starting with only 2 units, the investment is small, and I’m confident the market will respond well to this affordable product.

Revenue: After covering the cost of components and shipping, I expect to make a small profit from each sale. For example, selling the unit with the firmware installed at £49 (including shipping) will generate a profit of around £7 per unit.

Expanding the Business

Once the initial two units are sold and I’ve made my first profits, I’ll reinvest that money into purchasing additional stock. As sales grow, I’ll also consider adding other products, bundles, or more premium versions to expand the range. This will help me reach a larger customer base and build the business further.
